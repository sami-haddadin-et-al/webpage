## Biography
Dr Luis Figueredo is a research fellow at the Munich School of Robotics and Machine Intelligence (MSRM) at the Technical University of Munich (TUM). 
Dr Figueredo completed his PhD at the University of Brasilia (UnB), Brazil, with an awarded thesis on (dual)quaternion algebraic tools to robotics modelling and control. 
During his PhD, he also worked at the CSAIL - Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) where he received multiple awards for robotic demonstrations, e.g., Winner of the Rethink Robotics’ IROS Contest for best demonstrations (IROS-14) and a system demonstration award at ICAPS-14. 
After his PhD, he also worked as a fixed-term lecturer at UnB, where he was honoured as a guest speaker for the Mechatronics-17 class, and as a project manager for a GNSS-INS venture between UnB and Glonass, and as a researcher at the Federal University of Minas Gerais. In 2018, Dr Figueredo was awarded the prestigious Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions–Individual Fellowship, and moved to the University of Leeds, UK. 
During the MSCA-IF tenure, he organized the largest UK meeting on robotic manipulation and headed collaborations with sensory-motor/biomechanics specialists to design muscular-informed and ergonomics-based planning strategies for pHRC.

The research activities of Dr Figueredo spam to different areas of control theory and robotics with particular emphasis on cooperative manipulation, human-robot collaboration and algebraic and geometric methods to control and robotics. 
His research also focuses on interdisciplinary methods and tools to address large and unsolved problems.


## Research Interests
- Geometric Methods for Manipulation Planning and Control
- Manipulation of Complex Objects
- Cooperative (and bimanual) Manipulation
- Safe/Constrained Real-time Manipulation
- Safe Human-Robot Interaction
- Implicit and Explicit Human-Robot Communication/Collaboration
- Biomechanics-Aware Assistive Robotics
- Task exploration/understanding and Failure Recognition
- Control Theory with emphasis in Optimal Control, Robust Control, Time-delayed systems and Control in Lie Groups and Riemannian Manifolds
